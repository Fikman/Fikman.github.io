Suratul Fathia

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ (1)

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ (2)

الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ (3)

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ (4)

إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ (5)

اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ (6)

صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ (7)

Translation

(1) In the Name of Allah—the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful.

(2) All praise is for Allah—Lord of all worlds,

(3) the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful,

(4) Master of the Day of Judgment.

(5) You ˹alone˺ we worship and You ˹alone˺ we ask for help.

(6) Guide us along the Straight Path,

(7) the Path of those You have blessed—not those You are displeased with, or those who are astray

Today Hadith

Hadith 1 of Imam an-Nawawi (Actions & Intention)

عَنْ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَبِي حَفْصٍ عُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ رَضِيَ اللهُ عَنْهُ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم يَقُولُ:"إِنَّمَا الأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ، وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى، فَمَنْ كَانَتْ هِجْرَتُهُ إِلَى اللهِ وَرَسُولِهِ فَهِجْرَتُهُ إِلَى اللهِ وَرَسُولِهِ، وَمَنْ كَانَتْ هِجْرَتُهُ لِدُنْيَا يُصِيبُهَا أَوْ امْرَأَةٍ يَنْكِحُهَا فَهِجْرَتُهُ إِلَى مَا هَاجَرَ إِلَيْهِ"

Actions are but by intentions and every man shall have only that which he intended. Thus he whose migration was for Allaah and His Messenger, his migration was for Allaah and His Messenger, and he whose migration was to achieve some worldly benefit or to take some woman in marriage, his migration was for that for which he migrated.

Upcoming Events

The Hajj Pilgrimage

The Hajj is a sacred annual pilgrimage to Mecca, Saudi Arabia, required at least once in a lifetime for every able-bodied Muslim who can afford it. As one of the Five Pillars of Islam, it serves as a profound spiritual cleansing and a demonstration of Muslim unity. Pilgrims wear simple white garments called Ihram, shedding social distinctions to stand equal before God while performing rituals that honor the legacy of Prophet Ibraham.

The Tentative date for the commencement of Hajj May 17

The Eid Al-Adha Festival

Eid al-Adha, the "Festival of Sacrifice," is one of Islam's most sacred holidays. It honors Prophet Ibrahim’s profound devotion and willingness to sacrifice his son in obedience to Allah. Muslims worldwide celebrate by performing special prayers, wearing fine clothing, and ethically sacrificing livestock. The meat is traditionally divided into three parts: one for the family, one for friends, and a significant portion for those in need, emphasizing charity and community.

The Tentative date for the Eld Al-Adha May 27

Past Events

The Ramadan Fasting

Ramadan is the holiest month in Islam, defined by a dawn-to-sunset fast known as Sawm. Beyond abstaining from food and drink, it is a period of deep spiritual reflection, self-discipline, and increased prayer. By experiencing hunger, believers cultivate empathy for the underprivileged and strengthen their devotion to God. The fast is broken daily with Iftar, a communal meal that fosters unity, gratitude, and a renewed focus on charity.

The Eid Al-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr, the "Festival of Breaking the Fast," marks the joyous conclusion of Ramadan. It is a time of deep gratitude, beginning with communal prayers at dawn. Muslims celebrate by wearing new clothes, sharing decadent meals, and giving Zakat al-Fitr to those in need before going to the Eid. This "Sweet Eid" is defined by family gatherings, gift-giving, and a sense of spiritual renewal, emphasizing unity and compassion across the global community.
